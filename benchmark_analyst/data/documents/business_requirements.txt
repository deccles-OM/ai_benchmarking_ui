FORD QUALITY IMPROVEMENT INITIATIVE
Business Requirements Document
Version: 2.1

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
The Ford Warranty Claims Reduction Initiative aims to reduce warranty claims by 25% within 18 months through data-driven quality improvements and root cause analysis.

PRIMARY OBJECTIVES
1. Identify Top 3 Quality Issues - Reduce claim frequency for top 3 problematic components
2. Regional Quality Variance - Analyze why certain regions/dealers have higher claims
3. Predictive Maintenance - Develop early warning system for potential failures
4. Cost Optimization - Reduce warranty costs while maintaining customer satisfaction
5. Data-Driven Decision Making - Implement analytics-based quality processes

KEY METRICS
- Target: Reduce claims by 25%
- Timeline: 18 months
- Expected Cost Savings: $50M annually
- Customer Satisfaction: Maintain or improve NPS score

SUCCESS CRITERIA
- Real-time dashboard showing warranty metrics
- Automated alerts for quality anomalies
- Monthly quality trend reports
- Predictive failure identification with >80% accuracy
- Root cause analysis for top 10 issues

REQUIRED DATA SOURCES
1. Warranty Claims Database - historical data with repair codes
2. Vehicle Telemetry - real-time sensor data
3. Dealership Service Records - dealer-specific patterns
4. Parts Inventory - failure rate correlation
5. Vehicle Production Data - manufacturing batch tracking

ANALYTICAL APPROACH
- Hypothesis testing on regional differences
- Correlation analysis: production quality vs. warranty
- Time series analysis for trend identification
- Clustering for similar failure patterns
- Predictive modeling for early warning

DELIVERABLES
1. Initial analysis report (Month 1)
2. Root cause identification (Month 3)
3. Predictive model deployment (Month 6)
4. Real-time monitoring dashboard (Month 9)
5. Quarterly reviews and optimization (Ongoing)
