ANALYTICAL OBJECTIVES & DATA REQUIREMENTS

OBJECTIVE 1: Predict Quality Issues Before They Occur
Questions the system must answer:
1. Which vehicle models are at highest risk of failure in specific mileage ranges?
   Data Needed: warranty_claims(mileage, model, failure_code, claim_date), vehicle_telemetry(engine_temp, battery_temp)
   
2. Are there seasonal patterns in failures?
   Data Needed: warranty_claims(claim_date), sales_data(units_sold, year), historical_data(temperature)
   
3. Which dealers consistently have better quality?
   Data Needed: dealer_claims(claims_per_month, region, dealer_id), sales_data(units_sold), vehicle_production_data
   
4. What is the correlation between manufacturing batch and failure rate?
   Data Needed: vehicle_production_data(batch_id, manufacturing_date), warranty_claims(model, failure_date)
   
5. Can we predict failure 30 days in advance using telemetry?
   Data Needed: vehicle_telemetry(engine_temp, battery_temp, speed), warranty_claims(failure_code, claim_date)

OBJECTIVE 2: Understand Regional Quality Variance
Questions:
1. Why does region EU have 2x warranty claims vs US?
2. Which components fail more in specific regions?
3. Are dealer practices contributing to quality issues?
4. Climate impact on failure rates?

Data Needed: warranty_claims, dealer_claims, regional_weather_data, dealer_service_records

OBJECTIVE 3: Cost Optimization
Questions:
1. Which repairs are most expensive and why?
2. Can we prevent high-cost failures?
3. Are certain dealers charging more for same repairs?
4. Geographic cost variation analysis?

Data Needed: warranty_claims(repair_cost), service_bulletins, parts_costs, labor_rates

OBJECTIVE 4: Customer Satisfaction Correlation
Questions:
1. How do warranty experiences affect customer satisfaction?
2. Do quick repairs improve NPS?
3. Which models have best service experience?

Data Needed: warranty_claims, customer_nps_data, claim_resolution_time, customer_surveys

OBJECTIVE 5: Predictive Maintenance & Recalls
Questions:
1. Can we identify recall candidates early?
2. Which components warrant design redesign?
3. Are there safety-critical failure patterns?

Data Needed: vehicle_telemetry, NHTSA_safety_data, engineering_specs, failure_patterns

REQUIRED DATA FIELDS MAPPING
For each question, these fields are essential:

Question 1 (Failure Risk by Model/Mileage):
- warranty_claims: claim_id, model, mileage, failure_code, claim_date
- vehicle_telemetry: time, engine_temp, battery_temp
- vehicle_master: model, year, engine_type, transmission_type

Question 2 (Seasonal Patterns):
- warranty_claims: claim_date, model, failure_code
- sales_data: year, month, units_sold
- weather_data: temperature, precipitation

Question 3 (Dealer Quality):
- dealer_claims: dealer_id, claims_per_month, region
- vehicle_sales: dealer_sold_units
- service_data: dealer_turnaround_time, first_time_fix_rate

Question 4 (Manufacturing Batch Correlation):
- vehicle_production: batch_id, production_date, facility
- warranty_claims: vin, model, failure_date
- quality_metrics: defect_rate_by_batch

Question 5 (Telemetry Prediction):
- vehicle_telemetry: vehicle_id, timestamp, engine_temp, battery_temp, speed, error_codes
- warranty_claims: vehicle_id, failure_code, claim_date
- service_bulletins: known_failure_patterns

CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS
1. Data must be accurate and timely
2. Historical data retention: minimum 5 years
3. Real-time data ingestion for telemetry
4. Cross-system data reconciliation
5. Regular data quality audits
